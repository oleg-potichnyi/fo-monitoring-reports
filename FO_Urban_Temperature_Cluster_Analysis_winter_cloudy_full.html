Conclusion¶

A temperature difference of 0.71°C was observed between the warmest and coldest spatial clusters. This gap is consistent throughout the day and significantly exceeds the 0.5°C homogeneity threshold.

Therefore, Wroclaw should not be treated as thermally uniform. Using a single weather reference point for the entire city may introduce systematic spatial bias.

Local Temperature Anomalies Between Nearby Substations¶

To identify potential local anomalies, we search for substations located very close to each other but showing large differences in their average controller temperature.

The analysis is performed in three steps:

  1. Measure the temperature spread within each cluster.
  2. Identify pairs of substations located within 500 meters with the largest difference in mean temperature.
  3. Visualize selected pairs on the cluster map and compare their temperature behavior over time.
